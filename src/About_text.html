<H1>How to use the H2FpEF tool</H1>


The target users for this screening tool would be Primary Care doctors; Family Medicine or general Internal Medicine.


This application is designed to but opened on demand as pop-up or launched independently. It is based on the H2FpHF screen from the article in Circulation 2018. The is also a citation for an article from a recent edition in the AAFP Journal where I (sadly and belatedly) discovered this very useful tool.


The user interface is designed to copy the look of the screening table in the primary document but with an added column for data entry to be feed to the regression equation.

  • You may calculate the risk score using either or both protocols.

  • You can calculate the risk score using either the <v>Points</> and clicking the appropriate check boxes,

    • once you have updated the appropriate selections then click the <b>Points Calculate</b> and the score will be displayed at the bottom of the column

    • the graphic is a nomogram to interpret the risk score (see below)

  • You can also calculate the risk score using the regression equation form the source article.

    • Hovering the caret over the data entry box will display a tool-tip that lists the range of values from the study data

    • The data fields accept only numeric data with up to 2 decimal values, and can use the arrows to move the value up or down one unit

    • Hypertension is not used for the regression equation as did not contribute any additional accuracy

    • Atrial Fib is a simple present of not present, defaults to <b>No A. Fib</b>, simple toggle the button to move between options

  • Click on the <b>Regressioin Calcuate</b> to calculate the risk score and will be displayed at the bottom of the column.

  • If you wish to reset the calculator the click the <b>Reset</b> and all entry options will be zeroed out.



The authors suggest that a Points score of:

  • <= 1 would rule out HfpEF (regression score of ~<=0.23)

  • while a Points score of >= 6 would suggest that the patient does have HfpEF (regression score of ~>= 0.93)

  • with scores of >2 (~0.23) and < 6 (~0.93) as requiring further consideration, testing, or referral.

  • Though cutoffs for the regression values were not given in the article, but I have estimated off the nomogram.


A brief report is displayed, and saved to the system ClipBoard as it may then pasted into a separate working document, perhaps your EMR, to record the screening values and score(s).
