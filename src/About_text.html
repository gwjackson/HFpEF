How to use the H2FpEF / HFpEF-ABA tool


The target users for this screening tool would be Primary Care doctors; Family Medicine or general Internal Medicine.


This application is designed to but opened on demand as pop-up or launched independently. It is based on the H2FpHF screen from the article in http://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6202181/ Circulation 2018. The is also a citation for an article from a recent edition in the http://www.aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2025/1000/heart-failure-preserved-ejection-fraction.html AAFP Journal where I (sadly and belatedly) discovered this very useful tool.

The HfPEF-ABA was subsequently developed to allow a simpler set of data elements just 3: BMI, Age, and presence of absense of A. Fib. The original paper; Reddy: Nat .2024;30(8):2258-2264, and a review in Saguil - Am Fam Physician. 2025;112(6):696-687. The Reddy article also has a link to an Excel file which includes the complete algorithm, the AAFP only lists the Log Odds calculations.


The user interface is designed to copy the look of the screening table in the primary H2FpEF document but with an added column for data entry to be feed to the regression equation. The HFpEF-ABA only requires 3 of the data elements, Age, BMI, A. Fib, these have a â€¡ (double dagger) icon for eacy reference.

  • You may calculate the risk score using either of the 3 protocols.

  • You can calculate the risk score using either the Points and clicking the appropriate check boxes,

    • once you have updated the appropriate selections then click the Points Calculate and the score will be displayed at the bottom of the column

    • the graphic is a nomogram to interpret the risk score (see below)

  • You can also calculate the risk score using the regression equation form the source article.

    • Hovering the caret over the data entry box will display a tool-tip that lists the range of values from the study data

    • The data fields accept only numeric data with up to 2 decimal values, and can use the arrows to move the value up or down one unit

    • Hypertension is not used for the regression equation as did not contribute any additional accuracy

    • Atrial Fib is a simple present of not present, defaults to No A. Fib, simple toggle the button to move between options

  • Click on the Regression Calculate to calculate the risk score and will be displayed at the bottom of the column.

  • And the third option is to use the HFpEF-ABA calculate button. You will only need to enter the 3 data items.

  • If you wish to reset the calculator the click the Reset and all entry options will be zeroed out.


The H2EpEF authors suggest that a Points score of:

  • <= 1 would rule out HFpEF (regression score of ~<=0.23)

  • while a Points score of >= 6 would suggest that the patient does have HfpEF (regression score of ~>= 0.93)

  • with scores of >2 (~0.23) and < 6 (~0.93) as requiring further consideration, testing, or referral.

  • Though cutoffs for the regression values were not given in the article. You can estimate the cutoffs for the regression score by using the cutoffs for the Point score and back referencing the regression value with the corresponding point score values on the nomogram.

While the authors of the HFpEF-ABA do not define any cut-off values for their scoring system.


A brief report is displayed, and saved to the system ClipBoard as it may then be pasted into a separate working document, perhaps your EMR, to record the screening values and score(s).
